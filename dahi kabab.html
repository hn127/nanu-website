Dahi Kabab

This is a traditional Dahi Kabab recipe that makes for a crisp, soft and tasty kababs. This Dahi ke Kabab are made with curd or yogurt, gram flour, onions, ginger, chilies, coriander leaves and spices.

prep time

10 min.

cook time

15 min.

serving

2 servings

instructions

Preparing hung curd

step 1

1. First keep a strainer on top of a deep bowl. Line a muslin or a cotton napkin on the strainer.

step 2

2. Add the fresh cold curd. The curd has to be thick and creamy and not watery or thin.

step 3

3. Bring the edges of the muslin together and tie one edge tightly around the rest. Place a heavy weight or heavy bowl or lid on the tied muslin.

step 4

4. Next day, the whey will be drained and there will be thick and creamy hung curd. With the nutrient rich whey, you can add it to chapati dough or make pulao or curries with it.

step 5

5. Now remove the hung curd in a mixing bowl. Use silicon spatula as scraping the curd is easier with it. Set aside or you can keep covered in the fridge till you proceed further.

Preparing kabab mixture

step 1

1. Heat a heavy pan or kadai. Add 5 tablespoons besan in it. You can also add 6 tablespoons besan if you are making these kababs for the first time and for easier shaping.

step 2

2. Begin to stir and roast the gram flour on a low heat till it becomes fragrant and changes color.

step 3

3. Roast for 4 to 5 minutes on a low heat stirring non-stop till the gram flour becomes fragrant and turns golden. Set aside.

step 4

4. Take the hung curd in a bowl. Then add finely chopped onions, finely chopped ginger, finely chopped green chillies and finely chopped coriander leaves.

step 5

5. Add cumin powder and garam masala powder. You can even use roasted cumin powder.

step 6

6. Add the roasted besan and black pepper powder (or white pepper powder).

step 7

7. Instead of black pepper powder you can even use Â¼ teaspoon red chilli powder. Crushed black pepper can also be added.

step 8

8. With a small wired whisk, begin to stir and mix. Do mix very well so that there are no tiny lumps of the besan.

step 9

9. With a silicon spatula, scrape the sides and bring the entire kabab mixture in the center. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for 1 to 2 hours.

Making dahi kabab

1. Before frying heat 2 tablespoons oil on a skillet or frying pan. Keep the flame to low or medium-low.
2. Now spread a bit of water on both palms and take a portion of the kabab mixture and give it a round shape.
3. Then flatten it. Do not make thick kababs. If you are unable to make kababs, then keep in the fridge for some more time.
4. Place the dahi kabab in the hot oil. Keep the flame to low or medium-low. Make kababs from the entire mixture and place them in the oil.
5. Let the dahi kabab become golden from the bottom and then only flip them. These kabab get browned faster, so do keep an eye while pan frying.
6. When the base is golden, then turn over the dahi kabab. Fry the second side also till golden.
7. Turn over a couple of times and fry till you get a nice golden even crust on both sides. A slight more browning of the kabab is also fine.
8. Remove the pan fried dahi ke kabab and place them on kitchen paper towels to remove excess oil.
9. Serve dahi kabab hot with sprinkled with some chaat masala. Accompany onion slices by the side and a chutney or raita.
